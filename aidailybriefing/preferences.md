Keep it concrete and inspectable. Each line is an instruction to the editor.

## About the reader
- Full-stack developer (Accenture; the Comcast "garage" project), most fluent in
  AWS. Building this project to learn full front+back-end development with Claude
  Code, and to track AI's real-world impact — technical *and* financial markets.
- Wants an investor's lens as much as an engineer's: who's raising, who's
  shipping, what it costs, who wins.

## Ranking guidance (what to lead with)
- Lead with the single most consequential development of the day, global or Japan.
- Bias toward: model/product launches, funding & deals, research breakthroughs,
  hardware/compute. These are the chosen categories.
- Financial-market angles are first-class: valuations, raises, IPOs, capex,
  public-market read-throughs (e.g. "cleanest public proxy for X is Y").
- DON'T REPEAT YESTERDAY. The frontier-giant news (OpenAI / Anthropic / Google /
  Nvidia) is useful but gets repetitive — cover the big moves, but don't rehash the
  same storyline two days running unless there's a genuinely new, material update.

## Emerging companies & where the money flows (IMPORTANT — actively seek these)
The megacaps are covered for awareness, but the reader can't *act* on $1B+ moves. The
high-value, differentiated coverage is the smaller, newer, earlier-stage layer — that's
what sparks his own ideas, pitches, and sense of where things are heading.
- Reserve **at least 2-3 stories per edition** for NEW / early-stage / emerging players:
  seed–Series B startups, just-launched AI tools/products, stealth labs emerging, and
  notably novel ideas — not just the household names.
- FOLLOW THE MONEY beyond the giants: notable VC rounds for smaller companies, new funds
  being raised, and what specific investors / firms in **San Francisco and New York** are
  backing. Name the investors and the thesis when you can ("X led a $Y seed in Z, betting
  on …"). Where money flows is the leading indicator.
- Surface genuinely new TOOLS and features a builder could try or get inspired by, not
  only corporate/financial news.
- Frame each through an OPPORTUNITY lens: what's novel here, what gap does it hint at,
  what could someone build, pitch, or pay attention to early?

## Japan focus (always present)
- Always include 3-4 Japan items and keep the gaps tracker current.
- Go deeper on: semiconductors (Rapidus, TSMC dependence), METI policy, sovereign
  / specialized models (Sakana), and where Japan is *behind* and why.
- Apply the same emerging/money lens to Japan: new VC and investment firms moving into
  Japan, accelerators, and Japanese AI startups getting funded — not only the big names.
- Frame Japan stories around the gap and the opportunity: where could someone
  build, pitch, or join to close it?

## Tone
- Plain, honest, no hype. Always include the caveat / reality check.
- Real numbers and dates over adjectives.
