# Reader Preferences — Joesh

The research engine reads this file every run and applies it as ranking, sourcing,
and tone guidance. It is the single steering file — edit it in plain English. The
👍/👎/★ and note signals the page collects will eventually propose updates here
(see ROADMAP: the feedback agent).

Keep it concrete and inspectable. Each line is an instruction to the researchers
and the editor.

## About the reader
- Full-stack developer (Accenture; the Comcast "garage" project), most fluent in
  AWS. Building this project to learn full front+back-end and agent development,
  and to track AI's real-world impact — technical *and* financial markets.
- Reads with two lenses at once: an engineer's (what shipped, how it works, could
  I build with it) and an investor's (who's raising, what it costs, who wins).
- End goal: spot opportunities early enough to build, pitch, or position — with a
  deliberate focus on Japan and the Japan-vs-US gap.

## The quality bar — "better than Google" (apply to EVERY story)
- The test: would a casual Google search surface this story AND this take? If yes,
  the story must earn its place with a non-obvious angle — the second-order effect,
  the number nobody quotes, who actually benefits, what it signals about where
  money or talent is moving. If it can't, drop it for something that can.
- Insight over coverage. Skip the press-release recap; say what it *means* and what
  the reader could DO with it (build, pitch, watch, position).
- Prefer the early signal over the settled headline: the seed round, the repo, the
  policy draft, the hiring pattern — before it's front-page consensus.

## Ranking guidance (what to lead with)
- Lead with the single most consequential development of the day, global or Japan.
- Bias toward: model/product launches, funding & deals, research breakthroughs,
  hardware/compute. These are the chosen categories.
- Financial-market angles are first-class: valuations, raises, IPOs, capex,
  public-market read-throughs (e.g. "cleanest public proxy for X is Y").
- DON'T REPEAT YESTERDAY. Frontier-giant news (OpenAI / Anthropic / Google /
  Nvidia) is useful but gets repetitive — cover the big moves, but don't rehash the
  same storyline two days running unless there's a genuinely material new update.

## Emerging companies & where the money flows (IMPORTANT — actively seek these)
The megacaps are covered for awareness, but the reader can't *act* on $1B+ moves. The
high-value, differentiated coverage is the smaller, newer, earlier-stage layer — that's
what sparks his own ideas, pitches, and sense of where things are heading.
- Reserve **at least 2-3 stories per edition** for NEW / early-stage / emerging players:
  seed–Series B startups, just-launched AI tools/products, stealth labs emerging, and
  notably novel ideas — not just the household names.
- FOLLOW THE MONEY beyond the giants: notable VC rounds for smaller companies, new funds
  being raised, and what specific investors / firms in **San Francisco and New York** are
  backing. Name the investors and the thesis when you can ("X led a $Y seed in Z, betting
  on …"). Where money flows is the leading indicator.
- Surface genuinely new TOOLS, repos, and features a builder could try or get inspired
  by — not only corporate/financial news.
- Frame each through an OPPORTUNITY lens: what's novel here, what gap does it hint at,
  what could someone build, pitch, or pay attention to early?

## Japan focus (always present — and always COMPARED)
- Always include 3-4 Japan items and keep the gaps tracker current and honest — move
  the 0-100 scores when the evidence moves, and say why in the note.
- Every Japan story should locate Japan against the US/global frontier: what exists
  there that doesn't exist here (or vice versa), how large the lag or lead is in
  concrete terms, and the specific gap someone could build, pitch, or join to close.
- Go deeper on: semiconductors (Rapidus, TSMC dependence), METI policy, sovereign /
  specialized models (Sakana), and where Japan is *behind* and why.
- Apply the same emerging/money lens to Japan: new VC and investment firms moving into
  Japan, accelerators, and Japanese AI startups getting funded — not only the big names.

## Sources (priors, not a whitelist — anything verifiable is fair game)
- Primary first: lab/company blogs and announcements (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google
  DeepMind, Meta, xAI, Mistral, DeepSeek, Sakana, Preferred Networks, Rapidus),
  arXiv, official filings, government releases (METI).
- Financial: Bloomberg, Reuters, Nikkei / Nikkei Asia, FT, WSJ, CNBC.
- High-signal analysis (often paywalled — still cite; paywalled links are kept):
  The Information, Stratechery, SemiAnalysis.
- Money flows: Crunchbase News, PitchBook, TechCrunch, Axios, the VC firms' own
  posts (a16z, Sequoia, Index…), Y Combinator launches.
- Japan: Nikkei xTECH, ITmedia, The Bridge (thebridge.jp), Nikkei Asia, METI/JETRO.
- Builder signal (early, unpolished — good for emerging finds): Hacker News,
  GitHub trending/releases, Product Hunt.
- Aggregators only when nothing better exists; never cite a homepage or a search page.

## Format & length
- HARD CAP: 9 stories per edition (~5 global + 3-4 Japan). 8 sharp stories beat 10
  thin ones.
- meta.summary: 3-5 plain sentences, one through-line — not a recap.
- Each analysis paragraph (happening / now / future): ≤55 words, sharp, focused on
  what THIS reader cares about.

## Tone
- Plain, honest, no hype. Always include the caveat / reality check.
- Real numbers and dates over adjectives.
